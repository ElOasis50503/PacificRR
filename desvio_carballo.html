DCB-01 23 June 1923

Primer Cruce de la Quebrada Tular

First crossing of the Quebrada Tular · timber formwork for culvert or bridge abutment · 23 June 1923

Caption reads: Desvío Carballo. Primer cruce de la Quebrada Tular. Junio 23, 1923. The Desvío Carballo (Carballo siding or diversion) at km 96½ was a new infrastructure project under active construction in mid-1923, documented across seven months by this four-plate sequence. A desvío is a siding, passing loop, or diversion track — the same category as the Cascajal apartadero documented in CAS-02, but here on a larger scale and apparently involving a new stream crossing as well.

The photograph shows the first crossing of the Quebrada Tular stream, a small watercourse that the new siding must bridge or culvert. In the foreground, two workers in broad-brimmed hats stand amid broken rock and excavated material; one holds a pick, the other a shovel. Behind them, substantial timber formwork fills the middle ground: vertical posts, horizontal planking in stacked courses, and diagonal braces form the shuttering for a concrete or masonry pour — either a culvert box or the wingwall and abutment of a small bridge. A third worker is visible at the top of the formwork, checking or placing material. The scale of the formwork — roughly 4–5 metres high — indicates this is not a minor drain crossing but a proper engineering structure.

The hillside behind rises steeply, suggesting the Quebrada Tular runs in an incised valley at this point, which is why the crossing required dedicated structure rather than a simple pipe culvert.

DCB-02 c. June 1923 (date partially cut)

Desvío Carballo — Excavación

Excavation gang in rock cut · c. June 1923

Caption reads Desvío Carballo (date partially cut from frame; likely June 1923, same survey visit as DCB-01). The photograph documents the manual excavation phase of the siding construction: a gang of five or six workers, all in broad-brimmed hats, working in a narrow rock cut. Two men push or manoeuvre a standard construction wheelbarrow; others work with shovels and picks in the tight passage. At left, a near-vertical rock face rises above the men — freshly cut volcanic rock, the debris field visible at its base. At right, a rough-sawn timber post suggests formwork or shoring has already been installed on that wall.

The photograph is taken looking along the cut, which narrows toward the far end where a fifth worker is visible in profile. The close confinement and the volume of loose material on the ground suggest this section of the siding route required significant excavation to establish a level grade through the hillside — a minor equivalent of the Corte Matamoros (MAT-01) but on a smaller scale appropriate to a siding rather than a main line.

DCB-03 4 January 1924

Desvío Carballo — Vista Panorámica

Panoramic view of full construction site · km 96½ · 4 January 1924

Caption reads: Desvío Carballo. Km 96½. Enero 4, 1924. The photograph is taken from an elevated position — likely the embankment of the existing main line — looking across the full extent of the construction site as it appeared six months after DCB-01. The view is panoramic, encompassing the cleared right-of-way, multiple construction elements in various stages of completion, and the surrounding forested hills. This is the first image in this collection to extend the documented period into 1924, confirming that the photographic record of the Pacific Railway infrastructure programme continued beyond the calendar year of the main survey.

In the middle distance, a tall timber falsework structure — a trestle or temporary scaffolding tower — rises above the construction debris, with workers and material at its base. Further along the right-of-way, the cleared path of the new siding is visible as a light-coloured strip through the dark forest, extending toward the hill in the background. The overall impression is of a well-advanced but not yet complete construction project: the earthworks are largely done, the structure at the stream crossing is partway up, but track has not yet been laid.

DCB-04 4 January 1924

Desvío Carballo — Falsework

Timber trestle falsework under construction · km 96½ · 4 January 1924

Same date as DCB-03, taken from a lower angle and closer to the primary structure: the timber trestle falsework for what will become either a bridge over the Quebrada Tular or a supporting structure for the new siding embankment. The falsework is substantial: multiple bays of heavy timber bent construction, the verticals and diagonals of each bent visible, rising to approximately 4–5 metres at this stage. At left, newly laid timber decking extends toward the camera — the beginning of the working deck that will allow workers and materials to reach the structure above. At right, the cleared hillside rises behind with dry-season deciduous trees silhouetted against the sky.

In the foreground, construction debris: logs, timber offcuts, and earthwork material occupy the ground at the base of the structure. A lone figure stands at left on the new deck, giving scale. At right, a pair of conical spoil mounds — material excavated from the foundations — confirm that the structure is being built down into the ground as well as up. The proximity to a thatched roof visible at mid-right suggests a workers’ camp or tool store has been established adjacent to the construction site.

Together, DCB-03 and DCB-04 provide a comprehensive record of the Desvío Carballo as it stood six months after the June 1923 commencement documented in DCB-01 and DCB-02. The project was evidently still underway in January 1924 — whether it was completed and brought into service shortly after, or whether further complications delayed the work, is not documented in this collection.
