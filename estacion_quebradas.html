QBR-01 24 April 1923 · Plate 50

Quebradas — Bóveda de Mamposteriía

Masonry arch culvert · plate 50 · 24 January 1923 · Alajuela Province

Caption inscribed at upper left: Quebradas 4/24/23 [sic — read as 1/24/23, 24 January 1923, consistent with the January survey sequence]; plate number 50 at upper right. The AtoM record identifies this as “fotografías panorámicas de un lugar en Quebradas de Alajuela” — panoramic photographs of a place in Quebradas, Alajuela. The subject is not the station building but a masonry arch culvert or small tunnel portal built into the hillside embankment alongside the railway.

The structure is photographed front-on, filling the frame with its portal face. The arch is a semicircular or slightly pointed round arch built in dressed volcanic stone voussoirs — each block carefully cut and fitted to form the curved intrados — rising from stone-coursed abutment walls to a keystone at the apex. The spandrel above the arch and the substantial wingwall extending to the right are built in the same dressed-stone coursing, the blocks roughly square and laid in near-regular courses. This is deliberate, skilled masonry construction — not rubble fill but intentional dressed stonework, consistent with the quality standards applied to Pacific Railway infrastructure during the government-completion phase (1903–1910).

At the base of the portal, just to the left of the opening, a small push-car or hand-pump trolley is visible — a maintenance vehicle, confirming that the culvert was in active service use and regularly inspected by trackwalking crews. The ground surface in front of the portal shows evidence of water flow: the culvert carries a stream or drainage channel under the railway embankment, essential in the steep terrain of this section between Escobal (km 49.7) and the approach to Concepción (km 58.0).

Plate 50, dated 24 January 1923, is the earliest-numbered plate yet documented in this collection and establishes an important datum for the survey’s directionality. The survey was moving southward (from San José toward Puntarenas) in late January 1923: plate 50 at Quebradas (km 52.5) on 24 April follows plate 67 at Cascajal (km 79.2) on 19 January. Both dates are consistent with a southward survey that moved through the lowland section (plates 50–71+) in January–April, then returned northward to document the Ramal Alajuela (plate 105, 30 May).
