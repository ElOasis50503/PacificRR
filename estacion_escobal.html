ESC-01 24 January 1923

Escobal — Línea Férrea y Torre de Agua

Station No. 23 · Ferrocarril al Pacífico · Alajuela Province

The date inscribed in the photograph's upper left corner — Escobal 1/24/23 — places this view on 24 January 1923, capturing the Pacific Railway's station area at Escobal during a period of active operation. The composition is structured around two dominant elements: the track, which curves gently into the middle distance where a flatcar and at least one other vehicle with personnel are visible, and a large elevated water tower at right, its conical tank mounted on a timber-frame trestle braced with crossed diagonals. Stacked beside the tower are what appear to be pre-cut timber sleepers or lumber sections, suggesting maintenance or construction activity at the station.

The station building itself — a low structure with an extended overhanging roof sheltering an open-sided platform — is visible in the middle ground, partially screened by vegetation. Dry-season deciduous trees, nearly leafless, fill the background; January falls well within the dry season on this side of the Central Valley, and the bare hillside at far right reinforces the seasonal context. The track surface and surrounding ground are heavily disturbed, consistent with grading or track rehabilitation work rather than routine operations.

Escobal, station No. 23 at km 49.7 from San José, lies in the canton of Atenas, Alajuela Province, on the long descent from the Central Valley toward the Pacific lowlands. The station was later declared Patrimonio Histórico-Arquitectónico under the same 2021 heritage designation that protected Quebradas (No. 24) and the Orotina terminus. The water tower visible here is an operational necessity at a point on the line where steam locomotives required replenishment before or after the grade.
