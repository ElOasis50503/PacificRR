COY-01 11 January 1923 · Plate 62

Coyolar

Station building beside the line · plate 62 · 11 January 1923

Caption inscribed at upper left: Coyolar 1/11/23; plate number 62 at upper right. The plate number falls between Alajuela (plate 28, 12 January) and Cascajal (plate 67, 19 January) in this collection’s documented sequence — but its date, 11 January, actually precedes both. This reinforces a pattern already visible elsewhere in this catalogue: the plate numbering in this 1923 survey does not track strictly with chronological date, suggesting either a master numbering scheme applied across multiple simultaneous survey parties, or renumbering during processing rather than sequential exposure order.

The photograph looks along the track toward a substantial two-storey station building set back from the line on raised ground. The building’s upper storey has a distinctive octagonal or canted-corner form with windows on multiple faces — consistent with a signal box, lookout, or telegraph office function above an ordinary single-storey station structure below. The lower storey presents a gabled portico facing the track, with a partially legible sign reading ...AL CO[...] across the fascia — likely the station name or a freight-handling designation, though the text is too fragmentary to resolve with confidence. A row of substantial timber posts, possibly the remains of an old fence or loading rail, lines the embankment in front of the building.

In the foreground, loose rail sections and timber sleepers lie discarded or staged beside the track — maintenance materials rather than active construction, given the absence of any work gang in the frame. The track itself curves gently away to the right in the middle distance, with low scrub vegetation along both sides consistent with the dry Puntarenas lowland terrain seen throughout the January 1923 survey plates.

Coyolar, station No. 31 at km 71.0, sits in a section of the line between Mastate (No. 30, km 69.3) and Pozón (No. 32, km 74.0) — the same general stretch where the Estanque de Pozón (POZ-01) documents the water-supply infrastructure for this part of the lowland run. The two stations sit only 3 km apart, both serving the agricultural hinterland of this section of Puntarenas Province.
